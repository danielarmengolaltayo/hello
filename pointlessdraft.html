Daniel Armengol Altayó
Pointless, 2021 (draft)
Perfomance
(Placeholder for the video documentation of the performance.)

This is my proposal of extreme absurdity: surrender my full attention to a digital bombardment for 24 hours straight.

Because our attention is limited, in the same way that a day only has 24 hours. Because we are fighting for the attention of others in a battle that wears down our minds and clouds our senses, running the risk of gradually cancelling our ability to respond successfully to those complex problems that threaten the survival of our species.
We live in times dominated by a new medium that demands new forms of interaction and communication. A medium that imposes such accelerated rhythms that it hardly leaves us room to breathe, digest or reflect.

It seems that in this scenario, absurdity has become the maxim that moves us, and that only those who take it to the extreme, attracting the greatest number of looks and ears to their discourses, will have the opportunity to influence the behavior of the social body.
Instructions for the performer

1) Sit in front of the paper, the computer and the black and red markers. Focus your attention on the first square delimited by the grid on the paper and start the computer's process (instructions below).

2) If the computer says "yes", paint the square you are focused on, using the black marker and move to the next square (left to right, top to bottom) until you reach the end of the paper.

3) If you lose your attention, stop the computer's process, paint the next empty square with the red marker and when you are ready to continue, turn on the computer's process again and repeat step 2.


Instructions for the computer

1) Emit a "yes" or a "no", randomly, every second.
Each print has space for 3600 (50 x 72) pixels, equivalent to one order per second, for one hour.
