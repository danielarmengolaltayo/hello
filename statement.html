Daniel Armengol Altayó
Statement
“I don’t believe in art, I believe in artists.”
— Marcel Duchamp

“Art does not transform anything, it does not change the world, it does not change reality. What truly transforms the artist, while evolving, transforming and completing his languages, is himself. And it is this man, transformed by art, who can, from life, transform reality.”
— Jorge Oteiza
Our existence depends on our ability to interact with the environment and transform reality. A shared reality in which our actions influence what surrounds us, in the same way that the environment influences our own behavior.

My artistic practice investigates this constant flow of influences in an effort to understand and build my private reality, so that from there, new ideas are born, stories that soak the material and infect the mind.

My medium is conceptual, and the form will be that which best encapsulates the intention of the message.

I am here so that the transformation that emerges from me contributes to establishing new patterns of behavior based on commitment, responsibility and respect for everything that exists.
